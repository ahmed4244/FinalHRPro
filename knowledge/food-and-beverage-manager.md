# Food & Beverage / Restaurant Manager (the second promotion)

Rung 2 on the [[hospitality-career-ladder]], the target at **year ten**. You move
from running a shift to **running the whole outlet** — its team, its standards, and
its P&L.

## The scope
You own the venue ([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]): hiring and rostering, food and
labour cost, supplier relationships, and the standards that must hold on every shift —
not just the one you're on. A force multiplier through the team you build.

## What it takes
- **Operations:** cost control (food cost %, labour %), inventory, scheduling, health & licensing compliance.
- **Leadership:** hire, train, and retain a team; set the culture; represent the venue to ownership.
- **Experience:** ~8–12+ years.

## Certifications & qualifications
- A **hospitality / F&B management diploma or certificate** (e.g. a Diploma in
  Hospitality Management, or a food & beverage management programme).
- **Premises / alcohol licensing** as the responsible person where required.

## Courses & training
- **F&B cost control and P&L** — food-cost and labour-cost management, inventory, scheduling.

## Projects that prove it
- **Run the whole outlet** — own floor operations for the venue across every shift.
- **Own the outlet's P&L** — hit cost and margin targets.
- **Launch a menu or service change** (or a new-outlet opening) across the team.
- **Build and lead the team** — hire, roster, train, and set the standard.

## How the decision is made
Sustained venue-level impact ([[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level]]): a P&L
you own, a team you built and kept, and standards that hold across every shift —
attested by ownership.
