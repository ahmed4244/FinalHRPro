# A promotion follows the work, not the other way round

## What I learned
At big-tech companies you are not promoted because you *could* do the next job, or
because enough time has passed. You are promoted once you have **already been doing
the next rung's work** long enough that the title merely catches up. The promo
packet's job is to prove "this person is operating at the next level today", with
evidence: what shipped, who it unblocked, quantified impact, and peer testimony
from people already at that level.

Three things follow from this:
- **Sponsorship is arranged early.** A manager (and, for Staff, a Staff+ engineer)
  agrees to argue your case *months* before the review cycle.
- **Keep a work-log / brag doc.** A running record of impact is what the packet is
  built from — memory won't reconstruct a year of wins.
- **The bar rises with the rung.** Senior needs consistent team-level impact; Staff
  needs *sustained multi-team* impact, calibrated above your own team.

## Why it matters
It flips the plan from "wait and hope" to "act now". Everything in the
[[software-engineer-career-ladder]] project — the courses, the certs, the
projects — exists to make you look like the next rung *before* the review, whether
that rung is [[senior-software-engineer|Senior]] or [[staff-software-engineer|Staff]].
