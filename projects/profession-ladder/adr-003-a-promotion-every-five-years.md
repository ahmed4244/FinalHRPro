# ADR-003: A promotion every five years

## Context
The brief sets the clock: reach the **next two promotions within ten years**. We
need a rule for when each rung lands so the app can place the milestones on a
timeline.

## Decision
One promotion every **five years**: **year 5** → Senior, **year 10** → Staff/Lead.
We treat five years as the *planning horizon per rung*, not a guarantee — the rung
is earned by evidence, and the timing can move earlier.

## Why
It matches the brief exactly (two promotions, ten years) and lines up with the
industry's own bands: Senior is typically reached around ~5 years, and Staff around
~9–12. It also keeps the app honest — a promotion is won by
[[../../knowledge/promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level|already operating at the
next level]], so the five-year mark is when we *plan* to have the evidence in hand,
not a date that fires automatically.

## When I'd revisit
If the brief prefers a faster early cadence (e.g. the first promotion at ~3 years,
which is common early-career), we shift the year marks without touching the rung
content.
