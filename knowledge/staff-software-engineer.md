# Staff / Lead Software Engineer (the second promotion)

Rung 2 on the [[software-engineer-career-ladder]], the target at **year ten**.
Staff is the first rung that is **not** expected of everyone — where
[[senior-software-engineer|Senior]] is a level you can rest at, Staff is a
promotion you earn by demonstrating impact **beyond a single team**. Time is
necessary but never sufficient.

## The scope (what changes)
Your work jumps from one team to **many teams, a domain, or the whole org**
([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]). You set technical direction that several teams
build against, and you become a **force multiplier** — your impact comes as much
from raising everyone else's output (standards, platforms, direction) as from the
code you personally write.

Will Larson's four **Staff archetypes** name the shapes this takes:
- **Tech Lead** — guides a team or area's execution (the most common entry).
- **Architect** — owns technical direction across teams in a critical area.
- **Solver** — parachutes into the hardest, most ambiguous problems.
- **Right Hand** — extends a senior leader's scope.

## What it takes

**Technical**
- Design systems under heavy ambiguity; build APIs that are **hard to misuse**.
- Own system health across teams; drive reliability and testing strategy at the domain level.
- Tie architecture to business objectives and make the tradeoffs explicit.

**Leadership**
- **Influence across teams without authority** — align people by explaining the "why".
- **Sponsor** senior engineers into Senior/Staff, not just mentor them.
- Multiply knowledge through talks, written strategy, and reusable artifacts.
- Own a long-term technical strategy tied to company priorities; help hire senior engineers.

**Experience:** ~9–12+ years (Google L6 / Meta E6), but gated on impact, not tenure.

## Certifications (supplementary at this level)
- **AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Professional (SAP-C02)** — designs for organisational complexity, migrations, cost. The single most relevant cert here for cloud-heavy orgs.
- **Google Cloud Professional Cloud Architect** — the GCP equivalent.
- **iSAQB CPSA – Advanced (CPSA-A)** — a vendor-neutral *software-architecture* credential (Foundation CPSA-F first) for the architecture track.
- **TOGAF** — if you head toward enterprise architecture.

At this rung, **your track record and published designs outweigh any certificate.**

## Courses & reading
- **Will Larson — *Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track*** — the definitive guide to the role and its archetypes.
- **Tanya Reilly — *The Staff Engineer's Path*** — the day-to-day operating manual.
- **Books (depth):** *Fundamentals of Software Architecture* (Richards & Ford), *Building Microservices* (Newman), *Site Reliability Engineering* (Google, free), and *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* to mastery.
- **Grokking the Advanced System Design Interview** (Raft/Paxos, partitioning, consistency); **AWS/GCP Professional Architect** specializations.

## Projects that prove it
- **Design a platform or shared service used by multiple teams** — something that multiplies others' output.
- **Lead a large migration or re-architecture** spanning several teams.
- **Own a multi-quarter technical strategy** for a domain and drive it to adoption.
- **Solve a company-critical, high-ambiguity problem** (a reliability/scaling/cost crisis) — the "Solver" signal.
- **Establish an org-wide standard** (API, testing, observability) that changes how teams build.
- **Grow other engineers** — visibly level up Seniors; run a design-review forum or guild.

## How the decision is made
Show **sustained multi-team impact**, not one big project
([[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level]]). The packet leans on quantified,
cross-team impact plus links to design docs, RFCs and postmortems; **senior-peer
and Staff+ sponsor endorsements are weighted heavily**, and the promotion is often
calibrated above the immediate team.
