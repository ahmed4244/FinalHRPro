# The Aerospace Engineering career ladder

The ten-year map for the **Aerospace Engineering** cluster. You start with a
bachelor's degree as an **Aerospace Engineer**; the ladder climbs one rung every
five years:

| Year | Rung | The jump |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (now) | Aerospace Engineer | you are here — a bachelor's, ~0 yrs |
| +5 | Senior Aerospace Engineer | own a subsystem end-to-end |
| +10 | Lead / Principal Aerospace Engineer | set direction across a program |

Rung 1 is [[senior-aerospace-engineer]]; rung 2 is [[lead-aerospace-engineer]].

## The axis every rung moves along
The jump is **scope** ([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]): an Aerospace Engineer runs
assigned analyses; a Senior owns a whole subsystem through design, analysis, test and
qualification; a Lead/Principal owns the **system architecture** a whole program
builds on — the chief-engineer track. As with any promotion, you earn it by
[[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level|already operating at the next level]] —
the components you signed off, the review gates you cleared, the program milestones
you delivered.

## The two promotions
- [[senior-aerospace-engineer]] — the first promotion (+5 yrs)
- [[lead-aerospace-engineer]] — the second promotion (+10 yrs)
