# The scope ladder — task → feature → system → org

## What I learned
Every published engineering career ladder — Dropbox, GitLab, CircleCI, the FAANG
level maps — tells the same story along one axis: **scope**, the amount of the
world your work touches. It climbs in four steps:

- **Task** — you finish well-defined pieces of work handed to you. *(Software Engineer)*
- **Feature / component** — you own a feature or a service end-to-end. *(Senior)*
- **System** — you own how systems fit together across a domain. *(Senior → Staff)*
- **Org** — you set direction several teams build against. *(Staff / Lead)*

A promotion is simply a lasting jump up this axis. The title is the lagging
indicator; the scope is the real thing.

## Why it matters
If you know a promotion is a scope jump, you know exactly what to do to earn one:
stop reaching for bigger *tasks* and start reaching for bigger *surfaces* — a whole
service, then a system, then something more than one team depends on. It is the
spine under both rungs of our ladder: [[senior-software-engineer]] owns systems,
[[staff-software-engineer]] moves the org.
