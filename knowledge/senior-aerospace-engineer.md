# Senior Aerospace Engineer (the first promotion)

Rung 1 on the [[aerospace-engineering-career-ladder]], the target at **year five**.
You stop running handed-down analyses and start **owning a subsystem** — an
airframe, propulsion, avionics, or structures component — end to end: design,
analysis, test, and qualification.

## The scope
You take components through the review gates (PDR/CDR), own their analyses, and sign
off with a PE license behind you ([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]). The team trusts
your engineering calls in your domain.

## What it takes
- **Technical:** strong in one domain (aerodynamics, structures, propulsion, GNC);
  fluent in CAE — FEA/CFD (NASTRAN/ANSYS), CAD (CATIA/Creo); can own a design–analysis–test loop.
- **Leadership:** mentor junior engineers, own design reviews, communicate risk to the program.
- **Experience:** ~5–8 years.

## Certifications & licensure
- **FE (Fundamentals of Engineering)** exam, then the **PE (Professional Engineer)**
  license — the credential that lets you sign off analyses.
- Working knowledge of **AS9100** (aerospace quality) and the relevant airworthiness standards.

## Courses & reading
- Master **FEA/CFD and CAD tools**; study **aerodynamics and structures**
  (e.g. Anderson's *Fundamentals of Aerodynamics*, Bruhn's *Analysis & Design of Flight Vehicle Structures*).

## Projects that prove it
- **Own a subsystem or component end-to-end** — design → analysis → test → qualification.
- **Lead a design–analysis–test cycle** for a component to sign-off.
- **Drive a design change through the review gates** (PDR/CDR) and its verification.
- **Mentor junior engineers** and own the design reviews for your area.

## How the decision is made
Show you already operate at Senior scope
([[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level]]): components you took through the gates,
analyses you sign off, a PE license, and peers who trust your judgment.
