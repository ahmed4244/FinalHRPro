# Shift Supervisor / Head Server (the first promotion)

Rung 1 on the [[hospitality-career-ladder]], the target at **year five**. You stop
working just your own tables and start **running a shift or section** — the floor,
the servers on it, and the service standard.

## The scope
You own the guest experience for your shift ([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]):
you open/close, assign sections, handle complaints, and keep service to standard. The
manager trusts you with the floor.

## What it takes
- **Service craft:** the full steps of service, wine/beverage knowledge, upselling,
  and calm under a full house.
- **Leadership:** train and direct servers, run a pre-shift briefing, resolve guest issues.
- **Experience:** ~3–5 years on the floor.

## Certifications
- **Food-safety / hygiene** — ServSafe or a Level 2/3 Food Hygiene (HACCP) certificate.
- **Beverage** — WSET Level 2 (wine & spirits) or a barista/mixology certification.
- **Responsible alcohol service** where required.

## Courses & training
- **Steps-of-service and upselling** training, and full **POS / floor-management** proficiency.

## Projects that prove it
- **Run a section or shift end-to-end** — open to close, to standard.
- **Train new servers** onto the floor and own a service standard.
- **Move a service metric** — table turnover, upsell rate, or guest-satisfaction score.

## How the decision is made
Show you already run a shift ([[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level]]): the
standards you hold, the servers you trained, a metric you moved, and a manager who
trusts you with the floor.
