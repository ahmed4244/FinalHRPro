# Senior Software Engineer (the first promotion)

Rung 1 on the [[software-engineer-career-ladder]], the target at **year five**.
Senior is the "terminal" level of the industry — the rung most engineers are
expected to reach and can stay at for a whole career. Reaching it means you have
stopped needing to be handed well-defined tasks: you take an ambiguous problem in
your team's domain and turn it into shipped, reliable software.

## The scope (what changes)
You move from finishing **tasks** to owning **features and systems**
([[the-scope-ladder-task-to-org]]). You own one or more components or a service
end-to-end — you design it, build it, ship it, and carry the pager for it. Your
team plans around your work because it is dependable. You may act as the technical
lead for a project on the team.

## What it takes

**Technical**
- Translate business requirements into a system design; know when a refactor is worth it.
- Write flexible, maintainable code and **lead the code reviews** — you set the quality bar.
- Own the testing strategy and the on-call/incident response for your components.
- Deep expertise in the team's stack, plus working knowledge of the systems next door.

**Leadership**
- Mentor junior engineers; run technical interviews.
- Write clear design docs/RFCs and drive consensus on a decision inside the team.
- Partner with another team when a project crosses a boundary — the first taste of cross-team work.

**Experience:** ~5 years is the typical band (Google L5 / Meta E5 ≈ 5–9 yrs).

## Certifications (helpful signals, not gates)
- **AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate (SAA-C03)** — best signal-to-effort: "I can design secure, scalable cloud systems."
- **AWS Certified Developer – Associate** or **Azure Developer Associate (AZ-204)** — for your cloud.
- **Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA)** — if your team runs containers / cloud-native infra.

## Courses & reading
- **Book — *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* (Kleppmann):** the distributed-systems bible; the Senior baseline.
- **Grokking the System Design Interview** (DesignGurus/Educative): the standard system-design patterns.
- **AWS SAA course** (A Cloud Guru / Udemy / Coursera) toward the cert above.
- ***The Pragmatic Programmer*** and **Google's *Software Engineering at Google*** (free) for craft.

## Projects that prove it
- **Own a service or component end-to-end** — design, ship, operate, iterate.
- Lead a **team-scoped project with several moving parts** from design doc to launch.
- Drive a **reliability or quality win** — cut latency/errors, add a test strategy, lead a refactor.
- **Author an RFC the team adopts**, and run its code reviews.
- Mentor a junior engineer through a shippable feature.

## How the decision is made
Show you are **already doing Senior-scope work** ([[promotion-is-operating-at-the-next-level]]):
a promo packet with quantified impact (what shipped, who it unblocked), peer
feedback from Senior+ engineers, and a manager who will sponsor it. A running
work-log feeds the packet.
