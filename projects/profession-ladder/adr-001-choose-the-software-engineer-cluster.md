# ADR-001: Choose the Software Engineer cluster

## Context
The final project asks each of us to model a **profession ladder** — the
requirements to reach our next two promotions over ten years, one every five
years. A ladder has to be anchored to a single job family ("cluster") before any
of its rungs mean anything. All five team members share one starting point: a
**bachelor's degree**.

## Decision
We model the **Software Engineer** cluster, for the whole team. Rung 0 is
Software Engineer (bachelor's, ~0 yrs); the two promotion targets are
[[../../knowledge/senior-software-engineer|Senior Software Engineer]] and
[[../../knowledge/staff-software-engineer|Staff / Lead Software Engineer]].

## Why
It is the cluster we are training for, its ladder is the best-documented in the
industry (public career frameworks from Dropbox, GitLab, CircleCI and the FAANG
level maps), and a shared cluster lets the five of us build one coherent app
instead of five unrelated ones. The full map lives in
[[../../knowledge/software-engineer-career-ladder|the career-ladder note]].

## When I'd revisit
If the brief requires each member to take a *different* cluster, or if we pivot
the app to compare clusters side by side.
